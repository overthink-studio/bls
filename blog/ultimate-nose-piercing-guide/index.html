Ultimate Nose Piercing Guide

on July 16th, 2019

In the past, piercings were quite rare, reserved only for punk rockers and tough-looking alternative people, but they're becoming more and more popular in the modern era. There are many nose piercing stores in cities big and small nowadays, and nose piercings can add a lot of style and flair to any face.

Many people are interested in nose piercings but want to know a little more before taking the plunge. This ultimate nose piercing guide will tell you all you need to know about nose piercing types, nose piercing care, nose piercing jewelry, prices, pain, risks, and much more.

What Is Nose Piercing?

A nose piercing, just like any other piercing on other parts of the body like the ears, lips, or eyebrows, is when a hole is made in a part of the nose. Jewelry, including studs, rings, hoops, barbells, and other items can then be placed into the hole to decorate the nose and face.

The interesting part of a nose piercing is that the piercing can be made through both cartilage or skin. Some types of nose piercing will involve the needle or nose piercing gun penetrating skin, while others will involve the needle going through cartilage.

Both men and women can have nose piercings, and it’s not uncommon to see guys with nose piercings. Nose piercing in men and women is very popular, with both male nose piercing and female nose piercing offered by piercers in the vast majority of cities.

Nose Piercing Price

Essentially, the nose piercing price range will generally hover somewhere between $20 and $100, on average. It will all depend on the type of nose piercing you get, and the nose piercing stores you happen to have in your area.

The smaller and simpler types of nose piercing will naturally be cheaper. Septum piercings or septril piercings might be a little more expensive, and the most expensive kinds of nose piercings are usually the likes of bridges and high nostril piercings.

The piercer you choose can affect the price of your nose piercing too. More reputable and trusted piercers will often charge more for their services, and you'll usually find that the piercers in trendy locations and big cities are more expensive than those in smaller places.

You also have to think about the cost of your aftercare; if you get a nose piercing infection and have to pay for medical care, the costs will start to rise. There's also the matter of paying for jewelry.

Nose Piercing Types

There are many different types of nose piercings. Here are some details on a few of the most popular options:

  • Nostril Piercing - Very popular and not very painful either, a nostril piercing is when the piercer pokes the needle through the side of the nostril. You can wear a nose piercing stud, ring, hoop, or other jewelry on a nostril piercing.
  • Bridge - A bridge piercing is one of the riskier types of nose piercing. It goes through the skin in the bridge area of the nose, and you can put barbells in it.

  • High Nostril - As the name implies, the high nostril is when the piercer places the needle higher up when compared to a regular nostril piercing. You'll usually have to use studs or pins for this kind of piercing.
  • Septum - The septum piercing is one of the more painful options, going through the area between the nostrils. You can put hoops and curved barbells into a septum piercing.
  • Septril - The septril piercing goes vertically upwards through the area around the tip of your nose, almost like an inverted septum piercing. It can be very painful.

Will My Nose Piercing Hurt?

Nose piercing pain is a major worry for many people, and the thought of pain from a nose piercing actually causes a lot of individuals to think twice about getting a nose piercing at all.

The truth is that pain from a nose piercing is a very subjective issue. We all have different tolerance levels for pain and respond to pain differently, so some people might find that a nose piercing hurts a lot, while others might not feel a thing.

The type of nose piercing you get will also determine how much it hurts. A septril piercing, for example, is usually going to be much more painful than a simple nostril piercing.

The piercer you choose will also have an effect on your experience, as more experienced piercers tend to have better techniques. There's also the question of post-piercing pain, so you need to focus on nose piercing care and cleaning to reduce your likelihood of pain or infection.

Nose Piercing Aftercare and Nose Piercing Cleaning

One of the first concerns many people have after getting a nose piercing is learning how to care for it and clean it. If you’re wondering how to clean a nose piercing, it’s actually very simple.

The first step is to ensure that the piercing stays clean. You should clean the piercing twice a day, at least, and be sure to always wash your hands thoroughly before touching your nose or the area around it.

Your piercer might give you some cleaning solution or simply recommend you make your own saline solution at home with hot water and salt to reduce of infection. If you get a nose piercing bump, that’s a sign that something is going wrong and you’ll need to seek medical care.

When it comes to makeup and nose piercing, it's wise to avoid applying any kind of makeup products to the pierced area as this could slow down the healing process or cause additional unwanted pain. You should also try to avoid the sun as much as possible.

How Long Will My Nose Piercing Take to Heal?

Again, the answer to this question will vary depending on the type of piercing you have, and there's also the factor that some people naturally heal more quickly than others. We all want our piercings to heal quickly, but the process can be a long one in some cases.

On average, a simple nostril piercing will take a couple of weeks to be fully healed. A bridge or septum will take much longer, with septums taking several months in general. Your piercer should be able to tell you more and give you an estimation of how long your piercing will take to heal.

Can My Nose Piercing Get Infected?

Unfortunately, nose piercings are prone to infection. We touch our noses often, so it's easy for bacteria to spread around this part of our face, and we breathe in through our noses too, so can inhale bacteria from the air around us.

Nose piercings are most prone to infection in the early days after being pierced, but infections can occur at any time, and that's why constant and thorough aftercare is essential. It’s always important to clean the area and try to avoid touching your nose throughout the day.

If your nose piercing does get infected, you’ll usually notice some kind of visual symptom relatively quickly. You might see a bump or raised area of skin around the piercing, or you might see some reddening of the skin as well. When this happens, seek medical attention as soon as possible.

Preparing for My Nose Piercing

The first thing to do when getting ready for your nose piercing is to find a quality piercer. You need to find someone who knows exactly how to pierce noses to an exceptional standard and with total safety.

Plus, if you’re looking for something more complex like a double nose piercing, you need to find a piercer who is up to the task. It’s also a good idea to find a piercer you feel you can trust and talk to, able to ask questions and get real advice on aftercare and what kind of jewelry to use.

Above all else, hygiene needs to be a big factor to think about when preparing for your nose piercing and choosing a trusted piercer. You want to work with someone who really upholds the highest levels of hygiene and safety to minimize your risk of infection.

You should also speak with your chosen piercer about the type of piercing you like and let them examine your nose to see if you’re a good candidate. Some people might not be able to get septum piercings, for example.

What Are the Risks Of Nose Piercings?

One of the most common risks of nose piercings is the development of an infection. As stated previously, the nose is quite prone to infections, so these kinds of piercings can be quite risky from that point of view.

Of course, with proper care and cleaning, your likelihood of developing an infection in your nose piercing goes down drastically, but it’s always a risk worth bearing in mind and one that could prove quite costly and painful in the long run.

There’s also the chance that your body rejects the nose piercing ring, nose piercing hoop, or other item of nose piercing jewelry you’ve chosen. Sometimes, the body just doesn’t want foreign objects inside it and will make it known.

A common side effect of nose piercings is the development of little bumps of scar tissue. These can go away quite quickly or might linger for longer, and they might even be signs of infection too.

In the absolute worst cases, people with nose piercings can develop septal hematoma, which is severe bleeding and bruising around the piercing which can get very nasty.

Nose Piercing Jewelry

The type of nose piercing jewelry you choose will depend on the type of nose piercing you get, as well as your own personal sense of style and preferences. There are all kinds of different nose piercing jewelry items to choose from, including nose piercing hoops, rings, barbells, studs, and more.

These different items of nose piercing jewelry come in a range of styles and sizes, and you can find one that suits you at your local nose piercing store. You can choose from different colors and designs too, and there are more options than ever before right now.

It's important to remember that some types of jewelry will only work with certain types of piercing. For example, barbells are great for bridge piercings, and hoops are well-suited for nostril piercings.

You also need to think about the kind of metal you choose. Some people can be allergic and reject certain metals, so it might be a good idea to choose non-allergic options like titanium for your initial jewelry options to stay on the safe side.

Nose Piercings Conclusion

All in all, nose piercings have a lot of advantages. They’re a unique and stylish piercing to help you express your personality and sense of style in a fun way, and there are a lot of different options in terms of piercing type and jewelry to choose from.

As we’ve seen, there are a lot of risks associated with this kind of piercing, but if you find a trusted piercer and practice good aftercare techniques, you’ll have a very low chance of encountering any problems.
