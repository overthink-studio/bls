Acne Scar Removal

The procedure performed utilizing the Spectra laser is called a peel. During a peel, a client will undergo several passes of the Spectra laser, which omits quick pulses of intense light. These pulses of light target the melanin in the skin, breaking them up for the body’s natural processes to flush out. During the process, the laser heats up the skin, causing it to contract and stimulate the production of collagen.

Frequently Asked Questions

Generally, a tattoo takes anywhere between 2-4 weeks to heal properly. It is important to follow your artist's aftercare instructions while healing your tattoo, and to keep the area moisturized and clean.

The cost of tattoos varies greatly depending on the size, complexity, and style of your tattoo. Generally, tattoos can range anywhere from $50 to $1000 or more.

The amount of money you should tip your tattoo artist is ultimately up to you, but most people generally tip between 15-20% of the total cost of the tattoo.

The legal age for getting a tattoo in Ontario is 18 years old.

Anyone who is not of legal age, is currently pregnant, or has certain medical conditions should not get a tattoo. If you’re unsure whether or not getting a tattoo is safe for you, consult your artist for answers.

Yes, tattoos do hurt. The level of pain from a tattoo varies from person to person, and depends primarily on the location of the tattoo, the size of the needle, and the type of ink used.

Before getting a new tattoo, there are a number of steps you can take to help you feel more prepared. Some of these steps include:

  1. Make sure to read customer reviews and look at your artist’s portfolio ahead of time.
  2. Schedule an appointment and bring reference materials, such as photos or sketches.
  3. Eat a meal prior to your appointment to ensure you have the energy for the procedure.
  4. Make sure your artist has a sterile environment, and that all equipment is being properly sanitized.
  5. Wear comfortable clothing that will allow the artist access to the area you are getting tattooed.
  6. Bring a friend or family member with you to the appointment for moral support.

No, tattoo ink does not stay in your blood. Once the ink has been injected into your skin, it stays in the dermis (the deeper layer of the skin), and does not enter the bloodstream.
